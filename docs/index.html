5eCollector Tutorial Reference
5eCollector
A Discord Bot which is based on 5e.tools monsters. Free and Open Source.
Tutorial - Basics
5eTools is a bot focused on collecting monsters from the 5e.tools Bestiary. To start the bot, run (needs admin permissions).
col init
Now anyone on the server can start catching monsters! Running
col me
gives you an overview over your user. This includes rolls and catches. Now we can start finding some monsters! Run
col roll
several times and react to the message of any monster you would like to catch.
Running
col me
This shows your remaining rolls and catches and their cooldowns.
And with
col me monsters
You can see all the monsters you've caught.
Running
col view (monster-id)
gives you an overview of any monster (not just your own!). The "monster-id" can be found to the left of all of your monsters. For example in
#12 Mimic [Cr: 2]
The ID is 12.
Tutorial - Groups, Chosen and Combat
Monsters can be grouped into groups where each monster can be part of many groups.
Create a new Group with
col group create (name) [description]
You can now see this group when you call
col me groups
And you can view a specific group with
col group view (group-id)
To actually make a group interesting, you probably want to add monsters to a group with
col group add (group-id) (monster-id)
A group can hold as many monsters as you want, but so that you can use it in combat it may hold no more than 10 monsters.
Combat works via something called "Chosen". You may have at most have one chosen group at a time. This group will collect "glory" as long as it stands undefeated in combat. Choose a chosen group with
col chosen (group-id)
If you have already a group as your chosen, this will remove that current group and replace it with the new one. To attack another player (or yourself) you can call
col attack (target-player) (attacking group-id) (stat) [speed]
What exactly is a "stat" or "statistic"? Each monster has different values for each one of the six stats: STR, DEX, CON, INT, WIS and CHA. Choosing a specific stat will ensure the battle will be held in stat, so it is important to know what your monsters excel at when attacking.
Starting an attack starts a longer battle sequence where the creatures fight until one side is completely exhausted.
For longer battles, it is advisable to use the speed modifier.
Tutorial - Advanced Information
If you are searching for a specific monster it may also be useful to look through all available monsters as follows:
col monsters
col monster (monster-name|monster-id)
Searching through all your monsters can be quite tedious at some point, which is why a number of search and filter options are offered.
Examples include:
Sorting by strength (descending)
col me monsters sort:str
Sorting by strength (ascending)
col me monsters sort:str -
Filtering for monsters with specific words in their name
col me monsters filter:dragon
Filtering for monsters with specific traits
col me monsters trait:underdark
All these sorts and filters can also be combined:
col me monsters filter:dragon trait:underdark sort:str -
Note that the same filters can also be used when viewing all monsters instead of only just your monsters and that similar filters exist for groups.
If you want some statistics on anything in the game, you probably want to call the stats command.
col stats (category) [start-index] [number of results] [order: +|-]
Here are all categories:
points: Ranks players by points
glory: Ranks chosens by glory
monsters: Ranks players by number of monsters
level: Ranks caught monsters by level
hp: Ranks caught monsters by hp
ac: Ranks caught monsters by ac
cr: Ranks caught monsters by cr
str/dex/con/int/wis/cha: Ranks caught monsters by str/dex/con/int/wis/cha
Tutorial - Advanced Management
There are a number of options you can do to manage your monsters.
col rename (monster-id) (new name)
col give (receiving player) (monster-id)
col release (monster-id)
col trade (given-monster-id) (receiving-monster-id)
There are also several ways to manage your groups.
col remove (group-id) (monster-id)
col change name (group-id) (new name)
col change description (group-id) (new description)
col change favorite (group-id)
col delete (group-id)
Tutorial - Advanced Combat
Monsters not only have statistics but also traits.
Traits become active once enough creatures of that trait are in a single group. You can browse and research traits with
col traits
and
col trait (trait-name)
Traits are highly powerful and it is advisable to build your attack groups around powerful combinations.
Another way to increase your monster's strength is to level them up. This is done by finding three monsters of the same type and combining them as follows:
col combine (monster1-id) (monster2-id) (monster3-id)
The new monster will have much more powerful stats and obviously be way cooler.
To fully grasp combat, it may be of interest that the basic attacking pattern is very simple:
The attacker rolls to see if they get past the armor class of the attacked.
If they pass the check, they perform the attack, making the attacked take damage.

All other complexity is introduced via traits.
Reference
Basic Commands
col help
Gives you a link to this page.
col me [view|monsters|groups] [filtering and sorting options]
Gives you an overview of your user with "view".
Gives you an overview of your monsters with "monsters".
Filtering and sorting options for monsters:
s:[id|name|type|cr|hp|ac|str|dex|con|int|wis|cha]
Sort by the given attribute.
-
Reverse sorting order.
f:[text]
Filter by a given text (case insensitive).
t:[trait]
Filter by a given trait.
Gives you an overview of your groups with "groups".
Filtering and sorting options for groups:
expand
Show monsters inside of the groups.
s:[id|monsters]
Sort by the given attribute.
-
Reverse sorting order.
f:[text]
Filter by a given text (case insensitive).
col roll
Roll for a new creature
col user (user)
Gives you an overview of a user.
col view (monster-id|monster-type)
Gives you an overview of a monster (by ID) or a monster-type (case sensitive).
Groups and Monster management
col rename (monster-id) (name)
Rename a monster.
col group (create|delete) (group-id)
Create or delete a group.
col group (create|delete) (group-id)
Create or delete a group.
col group change (name|description|favorite) [new value]
Edit a group.
col group add (group-ids) (monster-ids)
Add a monster or several monsters to a group or several groups. Monster-ids and group-ids may be written in quotes and seperated by spaces.
col group remove (group-ids) (monster-ids)
Remove a monster or several monsters from a group or several groups. Monster-ids and group-ids may be written in quotes and seperated by spaces.
Combat
col chosen (group-id)
Make a group your chosen.
col attack (target-player) (group-id) (stat) [speed]
Attack a target player with the given group.
col traits [options]
Gives a list of all traits
Filtering and sorting options for groups:
s:[monsters]
Sort by number of monsters with that trait.
-
Reverse sorting order.
f:[text]
Filter by a given text (case insensitive).
col trait (trait)
Gives information on a given trait.
col combine (monster1-id) (monster2-id) (monster3-id)
Combine three monsters of same type and level to a monster of higher level.
